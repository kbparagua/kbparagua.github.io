Stop Whining. We All Have the Same 24 Hours.

Aug 31st, 2013 | Comments

Since I’m paying 10 dollars a year for this blog, and since my brain is beginning to itch with stuff in between shitty and witty, I’m obliged to write a fucking article again. Yes, I said fucking without any reason. Deal with it.

I’m an ambitious procrastinator. Well, I guess most of us are. Dream big. Do small.

I’ve many things to do — suppose to do. But most of my time is spent thinking and stressing about how little time I have to do all those things. You can very well relate I know. We have many things to do. Almost 9 hours in the office, not counting the travel and shitty unnecessary surprise overtimes yet. Then, stupid scientists recommend 8 hours of sleep a day. 8 fucking hours doing nothing! Also, we need to allocate time for eating, peeing, pooping, walking, bathing, house cleaning, and other necessary things ending in ing. I guess an average person is spending at least 19 hours a day to do all his obligations. Then using my cutting edge algebra skills acquired from grade 5, I say that we are only left with 5 hours worth of free time. Not including weekends.

5 Hours!? Holy Crap! That is not enough to do all my important shits!

If we can only have extra hours on our day. Maybe an extra 5 hours again? Or even another 24 hours? For sure, everyone will be happy. Everyone can finally do the things they’ve wanted to do: their hobbies, hanging out with friends, studying, and even sleeping. Ahhh. What a perfect world it can be.

Oh come on! That is fantasy! The reality is that there are only 24 hours a day. Even Chuck Norris has the same number of hours. I’ve realized that we are looking at the wrong side of the situation. Time is not the problem. It’s our attitude towards this precious concept called time. Our constant denial about the fact that time is limited. Our continuous desire to do what is not possible given the time we have. Those are the problems. That’s why we are procrastinating. That’s why we are depressed. That’s why we are frustrated. Because we can’t accept the fact that we truly have 24 hours a day only. We are fighting an indestructible monster. Let’s just accept that it cannot be defeated.

But! A big But! Errrr. Wait. Maybe I should use however. However! That doesn’t mean that we cannot be happy anymore. Just recently, I found out that the rule is simple: Don’t try to squeeze tasks into your schedule. Squeezing means that there are no space for it anymore. It will just burn the hell out of you or you will just end up procrastinating and never actually doing it. Leaving you frustrated, depressed, and craving to kill those people who has so much time to tweet every 15 minutes. Rather, do the tasks that are POSSIBLE with your current supply of time. And just be contented about it.

You cannot build a plane in an hour. But I’m sure you can build many paper planes with that time. I think that can be fun too.

…and it’s 1:00 past midnight! If I just have the time to make this article more sensible! Sigh.
