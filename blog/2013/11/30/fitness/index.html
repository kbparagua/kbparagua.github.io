The Sick and Crappy World of Fitness -- and a Proposed Solution

Nov 30th, 2013 | Comments

I want to get straight right away; The spotlight of physical fitness is your body’s health. It is not about protein shakes and supplements, neither about P90X nor Crossfit, neither about TRX nor the latest Ab machine. It is about you.

Observing the fitness industry today, I assume that we are losing our focus. Little did we know, we are falling on these clever marketing stunts. Numerous companies are letting us feel that achieving greater fitness is impossible without their product. They also go to the extent that they are proudly announcing that they are revolutionizing the fitness world. So-called “Scientific studies” are also used to make the stunts more effective. And even worse, the one thing that I hate the most, there are specific organizations that impose themselves as the enlightened ones, the elites, the we-are-the-real-meaning-of-fitness.

Just look at all these shameful bullshits.

I’m not implying that fitness products are not helpful at all. I just feel that the community is already polluted with buzzwords and lies. And considering the pollution’s volume, I assure you that lots of beginners are wasting their time doing the wrong things.

About a year ago, I tried P90X. It was fucking intense and I felt very satisfied with it. For me, it was the best back then. But as adult responsibilities take their places — fuck it, my time and energy was depleted every single day. I can’t even finish a single workout! I became frustrated and eventually stopped.

P90X is a good product, it’s just that it’s not right for my situation. Then Bruce Lee’s wisdom hits me:

“…be formless, shapeless — like water… be water my friend”.

“Adapt what is useful, reject what is useless, and add what is specifically your own.”

source

Bruce Lee has a valid point. I must adjust. I must experiment. I must create my own style. I must come up with something fit with my own time and energy. So that’s what I did. I adjusted P90X in-line with my limitations. I added some concepts from my past experiences. I added exercises I’ve learned from my friends. Actually, if you think about it, it’s not P90X anymore, rather a custom product tailored specially for me. A perfect workout program for me. Up to now, I’m constantly adjusting my workouts. I adjust depending on the situation, my body’s reaction, time constraint, equipment availability, short-term goal, and other important factors.

That’s another problem in the fitness world. Sometimes, because of the craze generated by the public media we became too attached to products. We forgot that there’s no silver bullet to obtain physical fitness. We forgot how to experiment. We forgot to listen to our bodies. We forgot to consider our limitations. We have more to learn. But these marketing stunts and hypes surrounding the fitness world are interfering with our learning. Today, it is very hard to find someone who speaks about fitness without bias or hidden motives.

“You’ll be in an amazing shape for just 3 months… For only $10!”

“We are going to share to you the secret to 6 pack abs for just a gazillion worth of dollars!”

“Running is dangerous for your knees. With our product you just need to walk to achieve that sexy body.”

“Traditional sit-ups might strain your neck and not that efficient at all. Our machine solves that!”

SHUT. THE. FUCK. UP.

Fitness should be simple. No fancy shits and lies.

I want to cut those bullshits. I want a community that has no bias or hidden motives, rather just passion on learning and teaching physical fitness. I strongly believe that we need a community built for the people, not for marketing’s sake. Built by the people, not by corporations. We need a community in which there is no authority. In which the authority is the community itself. A community where everyone has a voice on what is right and what is just plain bullshit. And more importantly, we need a platform to encourage everyone to learn, share, experiment, and discover our own style of physical fitness.

That’s why I created Vanillafit.

No bullshits. No fucking products to shove down your throat. If you feel what I feel, then I must say that this newborn community needs your help.

Join me in this journey.

Thank you random fitness enthusiast.
